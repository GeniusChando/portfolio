How I Work

A repeatable approach that turns complexity into content that actually gets used

1

Diagnose Before You Document

I start by mapping the landscape — who owns the knowledge, who the audience is, what format serves them best, and what gaps exist. Skipping this step produces content that's technically correct but practically useless.

2

Research Before You Extract

I do my own research before any SME session. Walking in informed means better questions, faster sessions, and more accurate output. Engineers and product managers stay engaged when they're not starting from zero every time.

3

Peer Review Before Stakeholder Review

Every draft goes through internal review before it reaches a subject matter expert. Catching issues early protects stakeholder relationships, reduces rework, and keeps review cycles short.

4

Communicate Early, Escalate Fast

When blockers appear; such as unavailable SMEs, shifting timelines, or unclear scope; I surface them immediately. Early escalation keeps delivery on track. Stakeholders who are kept informed stay flexible when it matters.

5

Build for Scale, Not Just Delivery

Every framework, template, and governance process I create is designed to outlast me. Documentation that requires the original author to maintain it isn't an asset; it's a dependency. I build systems teams can own and grow.

6

Measure Impact, Then Iterate

Delivery isn't the finish line. I track content effectiveness through support ticket trends, user feedback, and self-service metrics; then use those signals to close gaps and improve. Content that isn't measured isn't managed.

Enterprise DLP & Endpoint Protection Documentation System

Endpoint Protector by CoSoSys → Netwrix Corporation

Senior Technical Writer

Context

Endpoint Protector is a cross-platform Data Loss Prevention (DLP) and endpoint protection solution covering Device Control, Content Aware Protection, eDiscovery, and Enforced Encryption; serving enterprise customers globally with compliance obligations across GDPR, HIPAA, and PCI DSS. Documentation existed in fragmented form across different locations, with gaps, inconsistencies, and no specialist to own it. There was no structure, no strategy, and no system for capturing knowledge that lived with engineers and product managers. I was brought in to change that.

Ownership

As the dedicated technical writer for Endpoint Protector across both companies, I owned the documentation strategy, information architecture, content structure, and delivery. Working closely with engineering, product, and support teams, I led the effort to bring order to fragmented documentation; extracting tacit knowledge, establishing a consistent structure, and building a documentation system that could scale. Following the Netwrix acquisition, I led the migration of the full documentation estate to a docs-as-code platform built on Docusaurus and Markdown.

Delivery

Working across teams, I drove the consolidation and delivery of a documentation suite that covered; amongst other things; a comprehensive administration guide covering all platform modules; a structured getting started guide designed to reduce time-to-value for new administrators; a knowledge base of troubleshooting articles and FAQs addressing the most common support queries; and versioned release notes documenting feature changes and fixes across server and client releases. I also collaborated closely with the marketing team; producing technically accurate feature articles and product content that the marketing team then used for blogs, LinkedIn posts, and campaign materials. Following the Netwrix acquisition, the full documentation estate was migrated to a Docusaurus docs-as-code platform; restructuring content for the new architecture while maintaining continuity for existing users.

Impact

Structured documentation was among the requirements in preparation for the Netwrix acquisition; and the system I built supported that transition. The content is live in production today, serving enterprise administrators globally. As Markus Zauner, VP Product Management, noted: the documentation became a structured, scalable system that improved knowledge access across the business and reduced reliance on engineering. Krzysztof Staszałek, Product Manager at Netwrix, observed that the work directly contributed to product usability and operational efficiency; with structured content improving user understanding and reducing support overhead.
