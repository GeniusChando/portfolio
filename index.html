Enterprise Docs‑as‑Code Migration & AI‑Ready Runbook

Span Digital → Apple Inc.

Senior Technical Writer & Documentation Strategist

Context

Embedded as a Senior Technical Writer & Documentation Strategist for Apple Inc. through Span Digital. I was part of a team of technical writers and engineers responsible for migrating a large internal technical documentation estate from legacy platforms (MkDocs, Docsify) to an enterprise Docusaurus system. This high‑visibility migration required preserving content integrity, navigation hierarchy, and cross‑referencing across hundreds of documents.

Ownership

Within the migration team, I contributed to source analysis, structural remediation, and pull request submission. I developed a versioned migration runbook designed to work with Claude Code (AI agent), providing step‑by‑step instructions, decision rules, and verification checkpoints for both human operators and AI‑assisted workflows. The runbook became the team’s standard operating procedure, enabling consistent, repeatable migrations across projects.

Delivery & Technical Depth

  • Collaborated on analysing and migrating complex technical documentation, preserving navigation structure, sidebar hierarchy, and content integrity.
  • Performed structural remediation including frontmatter configuration, image path migration, internal link auditing, and Docusaurus routing corrections.
  • Validated migrations against live environments using Vessel before submission, ensuring quality prior to review.
  • Managed Git‑based workflows (branching, forking, commit authoring, PR submission) on Apple’s internal GitHub enterprise platform.
  • Used command‑line tooling (gsed, fd, pkl, Vessel) and integrated AI‑assisted prompts to automate and verify migrations.

Impact & AI‑Readiness

The runbook made the migration process repeatable, self‑improving, and AI‑ready – bridging human expertise with AI assistance. This work is part of a broader track record of designing AI‑integrated documentation systems and knowledge workflows.

Due to Apple’s strict NDA, no live samples or runbook content can be shared. Detailed architecture and methodology available for discussion under confidentiality.
